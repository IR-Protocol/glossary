# Dual-Track: Rent and Electricity Separation for AI Services

## One-Liner

Subscriptions fail because they bundle storage and compute into one fixed price — the Dual-Track Model separates them like rent and electricity, balancing the math for everyone.

## Context

Ever wonder why you pay for an AI subscription and still get throttled?

The answer is simple. Subscriptions mix two separate bills into one.

One bill is for **storage**. Your conversation history, preferences, and project data need to persist over time. Storage costs are fixed — like rent. Whether you live in your apartment or not, the rent stays the same.

The other bill is for **compute**. Every AI reply consumes real processing power. More usage means higher cost, less usage means lower cost — like electricity. You pay for what you use.

Subscriptions bundle rent and electricity into a single price. Revenue is fixed, costs are variable. The math never balances from the start. Platforms are not greedy — the model itself is broken. So platforms throttle in secret. Light users feel their monthly fee is wasted. Heavy users feel they never get enough.

## Insight

The **Dual-Track Model [IR-DT-002]** separates these two bills.

**Storage track — rent.** Rent a dedicated cloud memory space monthly. Your conversation history, project data, and preferences live there. Switch devices, clear cache — memory stays. This is fixed, because storage space is fixed.

**Compute track — electricity.** Every AI reply consumes compute, billed by actual token usage. Use more, pay more. Use less, pay less. This is variable, because your usage varies.

Separate them, and the math balances. Light users no longer subsidise others. Heavy users pay for every unit of compute they consume. Platforms no longer need to throttle or downgrade in the background — costs are now covered by corresponding revenue.

But Dual-Track is not simply copying telecom's "monthly fee + usage." It is a structural upgrade.

In telecom, monthly fees buy "number retention" — keep your number, keep your contacts. In AI, monthly fees buy **memory retention** — switch devices, memories stay; your past does not disappear.

In telecom, usage fees buy "data transmission." In AI, token fees buy **cognitive enhancement** — every inference makes the AI understand you better, building a more valuable **Digital Personality [IR-DP-010]** .

In telecom, storage is an add-on. In AI, memory space is the core — it is not just storage, it is the foundation of the AI's ability to "know you" over time, and it accumulates, portability, and appreciates in value.

Let us run the numbers. Suppose storage rent is 30 yuan per month, and token price is 0.0001 yuan. A regular user consumes 500,000 tokens monthly. Under Dual-Track, their total is 30 + 50 = 80 yuan. The subscription they currently pay is 30 yuan. Does Dual-Track look more expensive? The truth is: under the subscription model, they only pay 30 yuan because light users are silently subsidising the 50 yuan compute cost. Dual-Track is not a price hike — it is making the bill transparent. Everyone pays for their own actual usage.

What about a heavy user consuming 2 million tokens monthly? Dual-Track total is 30 + 200 = 230 yuan. The subscription they currently pay is also 30 yuan — but they get throttled daily. The extra 200 yuan is not a "price hike." It is the cost of finally getting a friction-free experience.

**Rent and electricity separation** is the most stable billing structure in human society. Nobody bundles rent and electricity into a single price, because it cannot be bundled. The AI industry must return to this common sense.

Dual-Track is not a price adjustment. It is fundamentally balancing the math of AI services.

## Implication

The **Memory Chain [IR-MC-001]** is to the AI era what the social graph was to the internet era. Dual-Track is to billing what rent-and-electricity separation is to housing.

One solves who owns the memory. One solves how the bill is calculated. Two sides of the same thread: turning AI from a disposable tool into a **Digital Companion [IR-DC-009]** that stays with you.

When your memory is independent, portable, and never held hostage, you can finally trust the AI that carries it.
