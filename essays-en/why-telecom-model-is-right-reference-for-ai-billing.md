# Why the Telecom Model Is the Right Reference for AI Billing

## One-Liner

The AI industry does not need to invent a billing model from scratch — the telecom sector has already spent three decades perfecting one, and its underlying structure is a near-perfect match.

## Context

Think about your phone bill. You pay a monthly fee to keep your number, you pay for data by usage, you buy a top-up pack when you run low. If you suspend service, your number stays and your contacts remain. Recharge and everything restores.

You have used this logic for twenty years. It feels natural.

But here is the question: why has no one applied the same logic to AI?

## Insight

We reference China's telecom billing model not because it is "similar," but because the two industries are structurally isomorphic across three dimensions.

**First, cost structure is isomorphic.** Telecom has fixed network maintenance costs — base stations, fiber optics, data centers — and variable traffic costs. AI has fixed storage and model training costs, and variable compute costs per inference. Both face the same commercial challenge: fixed revenue against variable costs. Telecom solved it with "monthly fee covers fixed costs + usage billing covers variable costs." AI needs the same decoupling.

**Second, user mindset is isomorphic.** Hundreds of millions of Chinese users have been educated by telecom for decades. Pay monthly to keep your number. Pay by usage for data. Buy top-up packs when you run low. Suspend service without losing your number. This logic runs so deep it feels inevitable. When AI becomes "monthly rent keeps your memory + pay-as-you-go for tokens," users do not need persuasion — they need analogy.

**Third, market scale is isomorphic.** Telecom covers everyone. AI has the same opportunity — reaching 500 to 600 million core users across generations. This is not a niche premium market. It is infrastructure-scale mass market.

But we are not simply "copying" the telecom model. We are upgrading it structurally.

In telecom, monthly fees buy "number retention" — keep your number, keep your contacts. In AI, monthly fees buy "memory retention" — switch devices, memories stay; your past does not disappear.

In telecom, traffic fees buy "data transmission." In AI, token fees buy "cognitive enhancement" — every inference makes the AI understand you better, building a more valuable **Digital Personality [IR-DP-010]** .

In telecom, storage is an add-on for files. In AI, memory space is the core service — it is not just storage, it is the foundation of the AI's ability to "know you" over time.

This is not a translation — it is an upgrade.

## Implication

For the AI industry, this is crucial. China's telecom sector has spent 20 to 30 years validating "fixed fee + usage-based billing" across hundreds of millions of users. It is the world's most mature "infrastructure billing" market.

The AI Memory Chain connects this logic and upgrades it for the AI era.

For domestic adoption, it offers the lowest cognitive cost to reach the broadest user base. For global expansion, it offers an "infrastructure billing framework already validated by hundreds of millions of users."

This is not about creating a "better subscription" — it is about switching foundations entirely. From "software subscription" to "infrastructure billing." It is an independent solution built on the world's largest telecom market, with Chinese institutional DNA and market validation.

When billing feels as natural as your phone bill, users will no longer ask "what am I paying for?" They will already know.
