# Why Subscription Models Cannot Sustain AI

## One-Liner

Subscription models create a fundamental mismatch between fixed monthly revenue and the variable cost of AI inference, punishing heavy users while failing to cover platform expenses.

## Context

Have you ever paid for an AI subscription, only to see a message like "You have used up your quota. Please wait a few hours"? You probably thought: "I paid for this — why am I being limited?"

It is not bad luck. It is a structural flaw in the subscription model itself. AI costs are not fixed. A light user may ask a few questions per day, while a power user may run projects, write code, and design solutions for hours. Both pay the same monthly fee, but the actual compute cost can differ by tens or even hundreds of times. Everyone loses: light users feel overcharged, heavy users feel punished for being loyal, and platforms bleed money.

## Insight

The root problem is simple: revenue is fixed, but cost is variable. Traditional software subscriptions worked because marginal cost was near zero — one more click did not cost the vendor anything. AI is different. Every prompt burns electricity, runs GPU cycles, and consumes real-time compute. It is not software — it is a utility, like electricity. You never see "unlimited electricity for a flat fee" because that would bankrupt the provider. The same logic applies to AI. Some leading AI services now consume over 120 trillion tokens per day, with usage doubling every three months. No fixed subscription can survive that curve. The only sustainable path is to separate storage from compute. **Storage** (your memory, your data) can be a monthly rental, like a phone plan. **Compute** (each conversation's processing) should be pay-as-you-go, like electricity. That is the **Dual-Track Model** — not a price tweak, but a structural fix.

## Implication

For users, this means fairness: light users pay less, heavy users pay for what they actually use — and no one gets secretly throttled. For platforms, it aligns revenue with real cost, ending the death spiral of unlimited plans. For the industry, it redefines AI as a metered infrastructure, not a disposable app. Technology should never force you to choose between being loyal and being valued. The Dual-Track Model gives everyone a clear, honest deal.
